Inclusion

Systemic inequalities exist within physics, astronomy, and the wider STEMM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics and medicine) sector which act as barriers to access to -- and progression through -- these disciplines for women, minority groups, and those with limited access to "science capital". I am committed to improving this situation through my work through my scientific outreach activities and through my active engagement in equity, diversity and inclusion work.

I provide talks or workshops on LGBTQ+ inclusion within STEMM, in particular, and speak at events focussed on improving the gender balance in STEMM. See this page for a list of events and activities I've recently been involved in. For bookings and enquiries, please use my contact details at the bottom of this page.

PRISM Exeter

PRISM Exeter serves as a network for LGBTQ+ individuals (and their allies) who work or study within the local STEMM sectors.

I founded PRISM Exeter back in July 2018 with support from employees and representatives from Network Rail, Exeter Science Park, Tech Exeter, the Met Office, and the University of Exeter. The decision to create such a network came off the back of a positive response to a stall I organised for the Institute of Physics South West branch the previous May.

In discussions with stall volunteers and other LGBTQ+ contacts within the local STEMM community, there was a sense of geographical disconnect between ourselves and existing company-wide or sector-wide LGBTQ+ groups. We wanted to provide a local network through which the discussions being had in different areas of the country could be continued and further developed within the South West and particularly Exeter and its immediate surroundings.

We aim to increase the visibility of the diverse range of people working in STEMM, particularly LGBTQ+ individuals. We recognise the importance of allies: for us, an ally is someone non-LGBTQ+ or not "out" as LGBTQ+ for whatever reason but who is interested in improving equality, diversity and inlcusion within STEMM. It is through working with (and listening to) allies - particularly those from other under-represented groups - that we can achieve our aim of being intersectional.

Institute of Physics South West Branch

The Insitute of Physics (IoP) South West branch coordinates the IoP's activities within the counties of Gloucester, Devon, Somerset, and Cornwall. I joined the branch committee as a volunteer in September 2017, intially with the aim of getting the IoP respresented at Exeter Pride. Our stall featuring hands-on, copy-at-home physics and astronomy demonstrations was the first time the IoP had been involved at a Pride event outside of London or Brighton.

I have continued to lead the IoP's involvement in Exeter Pride each year since then (although the 2020 event was postponed due to COVID-19).
