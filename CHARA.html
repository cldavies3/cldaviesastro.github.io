Center for High Angular Resolution Astronomy (CHARA)

The MIRCx collaboration

I am part of a team of astronomers from the Universities of Exeter (UK) and Michigan (USA) working on the commissioning of a new infra-red (J- and H-band) detector for the CHARA Array. This detector, called MIRCx, serves as an upgrade to the previous infra-red detector for the six telescope beam combiner, MIRC.

My role in the MIRCx collaboration concerns observations, data reduction, and documentation. During the combined commissioning and observing run of July 2017, I spent 16 nights at CHARA. During this time, we successfully installed the MIRCx camera and obtained our first interferometric fringes using MIRCx. We observed a handful of spectroscopic binary and "standard" calibrator stars which are helping us perform quality checks on the data.

I, and other members of the MIRCx team based at the University of Exeter will use observations of young stellar objects obtained using MIRCx to study the formation of stars and the evolution of dust grains in their circumstellar disks.
