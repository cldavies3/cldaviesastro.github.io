News Archive

Jan 7th 2019

Publication accepted

My paper entitled "Star-disc (mis-)alignment in Rho Oph and Upper Sco: insights from spatially resolved disc systems with K2 rotation periods" has been accepted for publication by MNRAS. A pre-print version will be available on the arXiv shortly.

Nov 26 2018

Acceptance of SPiRou proposal

The Taiwan Time Allocation Committee have accepted a proposal (on which I am a co-investigator) to study FU Ori and EX Ori-type variable young stars with the SPiRou instrument at the Canada France Hawai'i Telescope (CFHT). With the proposed observations, we aim to study the magnetic fields in these objects and their relation to the variable mass accretion rates exhibited by these stars.

Nov 20 2018

Publication of ESO Workshop presentation

Rather than produce a conference proceedings, the organisers of the recent ESO workshop entitled "Take A Closer Look" have placed PDF copies of talks and poster presentations. My talk, entitled "Grain Growth-Induced Curvature of the Inner Rim of Protoplanetary Disks" is now available to view here. This features work presented in my recently accepted paper entitled "Simultaneous Spectral Energy Distribution and Near-infrared Interferometry Modeling of HD 142666".

Nov 13 2018

Star Spangled Kyrangle Star-gazing event

For the second time this year, I led a group of colleagues from the Univ. Exeter's Astrophysics Group to the St Loyes area of Exeter for a Star Spangled Kyrangle stargazing event. The event was organised in collaboration with St Loyes-based community-based art project, Working With Gold, led by Clare Bryden. Even though we were plagued by cloud, around thirty locals joined us in the park and enjoyed looking through a telescope at Mars; learning about the starlore associated with the Summer Triangle constellations, Casseopeia, and Pegasus; and engaging with knowledgeable astronomers at our activities table.

Nov 12 2018

Co-authored publication accepted

Probing the Inner Disk Emission of HD 163296 and HD 190073, a study led by PhD student and collaborator, Benjamin Setterholm, of the University of Michigan, has been accepted for publication in The Astrophysical Journal.

October 23rd to 30th 2018

Observing at The CHARA Array

I returned to Mount Wilson to undertake 8 night of observations at The CHARA Array. On this occassion, I used the MIRC-X six-telescope beam combiner instrument to observe young stellar objects as part of a large survey of these objects. With these observations, we will be able to image the innermost regions of the disks that exist around stars as they form and search for time-variable asymmetric structures which could be associated with ongoing planet formation in these disks.

Talk awarded at ESO's Take a Closer Look Workshop

I received news that the talk abstract I submitted for ESO's Take A Closer Look Workshop due to take place at ESO Garching in October has been accepted. May 14th - 25th 2018

CHARA Observing run with MIRCx

As part of my role within the MIRCx collaboration, I visited CHARA to conduct observations of young stellar objects during the ongoing installation of adaptive optics systems (CHARA proposal 2018A:M5), the engineering of our new camera for the six-telescope beam combiner, MIRCx (CHARA proposal 2018A:M2) and for the 2018A:M4 program to study pre-main-sequence star binary systems. On UT date May 24th, with the assistance of Narsireddy Anugu, Aaron Labdon and Alex Kreplin, we successfully deomstrated the remote observing capabilities of MIRCx from the University of Exeter directly for the first time. This is a fantastic step forward in making UK-based MIRCx observations more economical and provides us with a training utility for future UK-based CHARA Array users.

Apr 13th 2018

Public outreach talk for Plymouth Astronomical Society

I returned for the second time to Plymouth Astronomical Society, this time to present a talk on "Star and Planetary System Formation". The talk outlined how stars form, how planets form, and what else constitutes a planetary system. I spoke at the end about some of the unanswered questions regarding the formation of high-obliquity hot Jupiters and how I intend to begin to answer these questions using optical interferometry. The talk, attended by ~50 people, was well received with lots of interesting questions.

Dec 21st 2017

Awarded time on VLTI/GRAVITY for P101

I have been awarded a total of 30 hours of time on the Very Large Telescope Interferometer's GRAVITY instrument as principal investigator during observing period 101 which runs between April and September, inclusive. Nov 9-20th 2017

Research trip to California

I attended the Sagan/Michelson Symposium at CalTech for two days of talks by past and present Sagan, Michelson (and a couple of Hubble) fellows. This was a great oportunity to speak with some of the best young minds in planetary science (and some older ones too). The rest of my trip was spent at the CHARA Array at Mount Wilson Observatory where I conducted observations of post-asymptotic giant branch binary stars with the MIRCx six-telescope beam combiner. With six telescopes, we should be able to obtain sufficient u,v plane coverage to reconstruct images of these objects from the interferometric quantities obtained. These images will enable us to study the circum-stellar and circum-binary disks which exist around these evolved stars.
