About me

I am currently a European Research Council funded Associate Research Fellow at the University of Exeter, UK, in the research group of Prof. Stefan Kraus. I am a member of the Institute of Physics and a Fellow of the Royal Astronomical Society.

My present work is focussed on the analysis of near-infrared interferometric observations of protoplanetary disks using worldwide astronomical facilities such as the Center for High Angular Resolution Astronomy (CHARA) and the Very Large Telescope Interferometer (VLTI). More details regarding my observing experience can be found here.

To analyse these observations, I develop and use the Monte Carlo radiative transfer code, TORUS, and the geometrical modelling pipeline, RAPIDO, to model continuum and line emission from individual disks. These analyses probe the innermost regions of proto-planetary disks, allowing us to study their geometry, composition and shape. From this, we can learn more about planet formation and disk accretion. More information regarding my present research activities can be found here.

Prior to arriving in Exeter in October 2015, I worked at the University of St Andrews, UK, as an STFC STEP Fellow following the completion of my PhD at the same institution in April 2015. My PhD thesis, entitled "Revolution evolution: tracing angular momentum during star and planetary system formation" focussed on observationally probing the efficiency of stellar spin down during gravitational collapse together with quantifying the circumstellar angular momentum retained within planetary systems. A copy of my thesis can be found in the publications section.

Aside from my scientific research, I am also involved in various public outreach activites. Details of these can be found in the outreach section. I am also a member of the University of Exeter's LGBTQ+ staff coordinators group and the department of Physics and Astronomy's Inclusivity Working Group.

Recent News

Aug 30th 2017

Approved to join the Physics Inclusivity Working Group

Previously named the Athena SWAN / Juno Committee, the Physics Inclusivity Working Group has expanded its remit to cover all Inclusivity and Diverstiy issues. Following a successful application, I will join the group as a new member from September.

Aug 12th 2017

Perseids in the Park outreach event

Joined by a group of four astronomy PhD students from the University of Exeter, I took around 50 residents of the Heavitree district of Exeter on a tour of the summer skies including constellations such as Aquilla, Lyra and Cygnus in the Summer Triangle and also Ursa Minor, Cassiopeia and Perseus. We were also treated to the beautiful sight of the Perseid meteor shower. The event was organised in association with the Heavitree Squilometre project. Credit to Chris Spinks for the photograph.

July 10th 2017

First interferometric fringes obtained with MIRCx

After a night lost to weather and additional software and alignment issues, the MIRCx team successfully obtained our first interferometric fringes on all baseline pairings of the six telescopes of the CHARA Array.

July 8th 2017

First light on MIRCx at the CHARA array

A week after installing the new camera for the six telescope beam combiner, the MIRCx team perfomed the first on-sky tests and successfully observed starlight on all six telescope beams.

July 4th 2017

Awarded time on VLTI/GRAVITY in P100

Reports are back from the European Southern Observatory and I am pleased to announce that I have been awarded 5 hours of time on the Very Large Telescope Interferometer instrument, GRAVITY to look at the degree of alignment in the CO Ori young stellar object binary system.
