Interests

My research is focussed on observationally probing how stars and their planetary systems form and evolve. I rely on high angular resolution techniques such as long baseline optical/infrared interferometry and scattered light imaging to study the disks that exist around stars as they form. These disks are the birth sites of planetary systems, comprised of the building blocks of planets like those in our own Solar System.

I primarily use data from the Center for High Angular Resolution Astronomy (CHARA), the Very Large Telescope Interferometer (VLTI), and the Gemini Planet Imager (GPI). I rely on complementary, open-access archival spectroscopy and photometry and have written and released SEDBYS, a python-based code which automates the process of building the spectral energy distributions (SEDs) of young stars.

I contribute to new and existing codes to assist my analysis of CHARA, VLTI, and GPI data. In particular, the Monte Carlo radiative transfer code, TORUS (originally develop by Prof. Tim Harries), RAPIDO (the Radiative transfer and Analytic modelling Pipeline for Interferometric Disk Observations), the MIRC-X pipeline (a suite of python scripts for the reduction, archiving, and visualisation of CHARA/MIRC-X data), and gpi-pypeline (a suite of python scripts developed for the reduction, visualisation and analysis of GPI data).
